Kurama (九喇嘛), more commonly known as the Nine-Tails (九尾, Kyūbi), is one of the nine tailed beasts. Centuries of being regarded as a mindless monster and sought after as a tool for war caused Kurama to hate humans. After being sealed into Naruto Uzumaki, Kurama attempts to maintain its cynical perspective about the world, but with Naruto's insistence on treating it with respect, the fox overturns its hatred and willingly strives to use its power for the world's salvation.

Background
Kurama and the other young tailed beasts with Hagoromo.

Kurama and the other young tailed beasts with Hagoromo.

Kurama and the tailed beasts first came into being in the waning days of Hagoromo Ōtsutsuki, who used his Creation of All Things Technique to divide the Ten-Tails' chakra into nine separate bodies and give them each a name. Sometime after being created, the Sage explained to the young tailed beasts that a link existed between them despite their separation. He also foretold of a day when they would be brought together again—though not as they were—and that at that time someone would emerge to show them what true power is.[6][7] In the anime, before his death, Hagoromo sent Kurama to live in a temple built for the purpose of protecting it in a forest region with mountains that would later become part of the Land of Fire.[8] Over time, Kurama gained a fearsome reputation as a natural disaster that can desolate areas where the darkest aspects of human nature collect and fester.[9][10]

During the time of the founding of the shinobi villages, the Gold and Silver Brothers of Kumogakure were assigned to capture Kurama, but they were both swallowed whole by the fox. However, the two brothers survived and gained some of its power by eating the flesh of its stomach for two weeks, forcing Kurama to regurgitate them.[11][12][13]
Kurama and Madara vs. Hashirama.

Kurama and Madara vs. Hashirama.

After Madara Uchiha's defection from Konohagakure, he found Kurama and used his Sharingan to control it, forcing the fox to help him fight against Hashirama Senju, the First Hokage. The epic battle left a colossal scar in the earth that would come to be called the Valley of the End.[14][15] After his victory over Madara, Hashirama's wife, Mito Uzumaki, sealed the fox within herself, becoming its first jinchūriki.[16]

Over the period of time that included the First and Second Shinobi World Wars, Mito kept Kurama at bay except when the seal nearly broke when she was going through childbirth. Towards the final years of Mito's life, it was decided that a new jinchūriki would have to be found, and that was another member of her clan, Kushina Uzumaki. Although Kushina was terrified when she learned of it, Mito comforted her young successor by telling her that by filling herself with love, Kushina would overcome the fox's hatred.[16] This was shown years later that despite Kurama's constant attempts to destroy her hope for happiness as its jinchūriki and coerce her to break the seal, Kushina's love for Minato Namikaze and his love for her were proven to be strong enough to contain the fox.[17]
Kurama, extracted from Kushina.

Kurama, extracted from Kushina.

Soon after the end of the Third Shinobi World War, the Third Hokage made special arrangements for Minato to ensure that Kurama would remain sealed inside Kushina while she gave birth to Naruto on the night of October 10. However, despite keeping the childbirth a secret, a masked man called Tobi tracked down their location, killed their escorts, and held Naruto hostage, forcing Minato to quickly rescue him and teleport him to safety. Right after Tobi captured Kushina, he extracted and seized control of Kurama and tried to have the fox kill its barely surviving jinchūriki, but Minato made it in time to save her. Soon afterwards, the masked man summoned Kurama into Konoha and ordered it to destroy the village.[18]
Kurama attacks Konoha.

Kurama attacks Konoha.

While Kurama was rampaging in the village, Minato fought against Tobi and soon managed to place a Contract Seal on him, which freed the fox from his control. Despite that, Kurama still harboured resentment against Konoha for sealing it away and continued decimating the shinobi forces that tried to repel it. As it prepared a Tailed Beast Ball, Minato summoned Gamabunta on top of the fox and quickly teleported it to Kushina and Naruto's location, letting its Tailed Beast Ball detonate away from the village. Already dying from the extraction, Kushina used her remaining strength to restrain Kurama, planning to seal the fox back inside her so it would die with her. However, knowing that Kurama would revive without a host and how great a threat Tobi was, Minato instead came up with a plan to give his son the means to combat the masked man in the future.[19]
Kurama's Yang half, moments after being sealed within Naruto.

Kurama's Yang half, moments after being sealed within Naruto.

Because it was impossible for Minato to seal Kurama entirely on his own, he first used the Dead Demon Consuming Seal to separate and seal the fox's Yin half within himself and then prepared the Eight Trigrams Seal to imprison the Yang half within Naruto.[20][21] Realising what they were planning, Kurama attempted to kill Naruto, but both of the parents used themselves as shields and completed the sealing.[22] Although greatly frustrated of being sealed again, Kurama decided to wait for the time when it could influence Naruto to use its power to break the seal.[23] Throughout the years, Kurama weakened the seal through the cracks of its formation by constantly sending out small portions of its chakra, which had positive side-effects on Naruto, such as amplifying his naturally large chakra reserves and healing ability, being able to heal from minor wounds within seconds to major injuries within a day.

In the anime, it was revealed that Kurama had left behind large amounts of its chakra during the attack. Kazuma, a former member of the Twelve Guardian Ninja, collected and sealed the chakra within his son, Sora, hoping to make use of its power for his own agenda.[24]
Personality

Kurama is characterised as a snarky and shrewd individual. It uses "washi" (ワシ) when referring to itself, which is generally used by older men. Kurama expresses great pride in its power, believing itself to be the strongest of the tailed beasts due to its belief that their strength is determined by their number of tails, which earned Kurama nothing but disapproval from its brethren, especially Shukaku.[25] The fox would also react out of jealousy whenever Naruto was not using its power.[26] Conversely, Kurama openly acknowledged the fact that it alone doesn't stand a chance against the Ten-Tails,[27] and from its past experience with Madara, the fox knew full well he was not an opponent to be underestimated, even when he was seemingly pushed into a corner.[28] Kurama holds great respect to Hagoromo Ōtsutsuki; during the tailed beasts' youth, the fox shed tears after the Sage had imparted his final words to them.[7] Kurama's manner of speech is often harsh, but deep down it has a supportive disposition, an ability to express concern for its allies and give some words of encouragement.[29][30] In addition, thanks to its exploits during the Fourth Shinobi World War and working alongside Naruto, Kurama's peaceful existence expanded to the Five Great Shinobi Countries, as it would talk directly to the other Kage and assist over important matters.[31]

Kurama also has a comical side; the fox brazenly teased Naruto about his first kiss with Sasuke,[7] argued with him about each other's stubbornness,[32] and nervously lashed out at a joyful Naruto after the tailed beasts were freed from Kaguya Ōtsutsuki while flipping him off.[33] In the anime, Kurama came to fear Himawari after being incapacitated by her Gentle Fist attack during an incident where Naruto had to shield Boruto from Himawari, who attacked him in anger for damaging her panda plushy.[34]
The change in dynamics between Naruto and Kurama.

The change in dynamics between Naruto and Kurama.

Due to centuries of being perceived as a terrifying monster or a powerful tool of war that must either be sealed or weaponised, Kurama developed intense hostility and distrust towards humans, even going as far as proclaiming itself to be the living embodiment of hatred.[35] Ever since its sealing within Naruto, Kurama plotted to use his dependence on its power to break free, becoming an ever-looming threat to both Naruto and the people he cared about. Even after Naruto managed to best Kurama in combat,[36] the fox tried to instill doubt in him about his ability to deal with the hatred in both the war and Sasuke, but Naruto was determined to find a way and hoped to resolve Kurama's hatred someday.[37] Despite reproaching him for making such a claim, Kurama was visibly shocked by the gesture, later choosing to assist Naruto against the reincarnated Madara and allowing him to utilise the Nine-Tails Chakra Mode without consequence during the early stages of the war. Soon, after listening to Naruto's conversation with Son Gokū about his sincere desire to save the tailed beasts and become friends with Kurama,[38] the fox, who witnessed all of the hardships and deeds Naruto had in his life, decided to stand by and see if he could truly aid its brethren.[39] When Naruto successfully freed Son Gokū from Obito's control, Kurama finally became convinced of Naruto's word and, in return, extended its hand to him as a form of partnership, to which Naruto then removed the seal that restrained it and happily acknowledged the fox as his teammate and a member of Konoha.[40]

Ultimately, Kurama formed a strong bond with Naruto; the fox proudly declared itself his friend and admitted that it had the utmost faith in Naruto's ability to carry on the legacy Minato and Jiraiya had left him, therefore abandoning its grudge against the Fourth Hokage for sealing it.[41] Naruto trusted Kurama enough to allow the fox to control his body and switch back without conflict.[42] When Naruto became notably distraught as Neji Hyūga died in his arms, Kurama fiercely reaffirmed Neji's sacrifice, reminding Naruto of his parents' similar sacrifices for him and how they shouldn't be in vain.[43] Later, despite being free from its seal, Kurama didn't turn down Hagoromo's request to stay with Naruto.[44] Kurama even went as far as to sacrifice its own life for Naruto, as the fox intentionally hid the actual risk of using Baryon Mode from him because it knew he would oppose it if he knew the truth.[45]

Kurama even came to care for Naruto's family, finding them entertaining to be around, further indicating that the fox saw it as its responsibility to look after them akin to being a parent.[46] Three years after its demise and being reborn within Himawari, Kurama displayed a rather mentoring nature towards the young girl, calmly explaining the situation and offering advice. Likewise, the fox appreciated Himawari's acceptance and respect towards it.
Appearance

    A young Kurama.
    A young Kurama.

1/3

Kurama is a red-orange coloured kitsune with black fur around its red eyes that stretch up to its ears, and nine long swiping tails. It also has an upper-body structure of a human, complete with opposable thumbs on its clawed hands.

During the last remaining days of Hagoromo, Kurama in its youth was much smaller in size, yet still towering over an adult, with leaner and softer while less-defined in shape with a rounder face, akin to a fox cub.[7] Over time, Kurama's size grew to roughly equaling the Hokage Rock, and Gamabunta, the largest of the Mount Myōboku's toads, was only the size of Kurama's torso. Its pupil alone was bigger than a tall human.[47] When Minato separated Kurama's Yin chakra and Yang chakra, it was split into two entities, both of which were shrunken more than half its original size, yet still roughly the size of other massive tailed beasts.[22][48] While the two were basically identical to the other, Yang-Kurama retained its original colouring while Yin-Kurama was darker coloured.[49][50]

While manifesting its Nine-Tails Chakra Mode, the fox's entire body took on a transparent golden colour, its whiskers were much thicker, and black circular markings appeared on its shoulders, stomach, as well as the front and back of its paws. Black lines also ran the length of its spine, arms, legs and tails. Additionally, while Naruto is in Sage Mode, Kurama gained similar thick dark patches circling around its eyes.[51] Upon gaining Naruto's Six Paths Chakra, Kurama's Tailed Beast Mode avatar grew to tremendous proportions, roughly the same size as Sasuke Uchiha's Six Paths Complete Body — Susanoo, with its tails becoming bushier.[52]

After both halves of Kurama were reunited, the fox grew even larger than ever before, to the point it was towering over a stump of a God Tree.

Three years after its demise from over-usage of the Baryon Mode, Kurama reformed as cub like it did when first created by Hagoromo, albeit much smaller, roughly the size of a large pet dog, with very long tails.
Abilities

See also: Jinchūriki Forms, Nine-Tails Chakra Mode, and Baryon Mode
Kurama's destructive power.

Kurama's destructive power.

Kurama is widely known as the strongest of the nine tailed beasts.[53][25] Hashirama Senju, who could subdue the fox, viewed it as too powerful and dangerous to be left roaming free.[54] Even with only half its power, Kurama remained strong enough to defeat five other tailed beasts simultaneously.[55] After reuniting its Yin and Yang halves, the absolute peak of Kurama's power was enough to decisively overwhelm Isshiki Ōtsutsuki.
Chakra and Physical Prowess
Kurama's chakra is enough to power up an entire shinobi army.

Kurama's chakra is enough to power up an entire shinobi army.

Kurama possesses vast and powerful chakra, the most of the nine tailed beasts.[56] It could be passed on to the entire Allied Shinobi Forces, be felt countries away by non-sensors,[57] and was required to be sealed last into the Demonic Statue of the Outer Path to avoid overloading it.[58] It can also accumulate a huge amount of chakra shortly after it has used up most of its own. Due to Naruto's compatibility with Kurama's chakra, any chakra it possessed is limited for its jinchūriki alone, unless it was moulded to fit the recipient's unique chakra signature.[59]

When used by others, the chakra became dense enough to be seen as a visible shroud that can further protect the wearer. Using its chakra, Kurama could shroud itself within its Nine-Tails Chakra Mode to increase its power. As a last-ditch ability, Kurama could enter Baryon Mode. In this state, similar to nuclear fusion, Kurama's chakra was consumed as raw materials to produce a new type of energy. When in use, it dramatically boosted Kurama's reflexes, raw might, and chakra to extreme levels. Because all chakra is connected, the same power that is produced from bits of their lives also works to reduce the lifespan of opponents on contact. Due to its extreme energy consumption, Kurama must remain focused and not waste energy of extraneous thoughts and actions, to maximise the amount of time Baryon Mode can be maintained.[60] As a tailed beast, Kurama has powerful healing capacities, able to mend injuries in itself and others in seconds.[61] Likewise as such, it is effectively immortal. When it dies, it will eventually revive, albeit in a younger form.

Kurama has a great deal of brute force, reputedly able to raise tsunamis and flatten mountains with just a single swipe of a tail.[62] Even with half its strength, it could overpower five of Obito's controlled tailed beasts,[55] smash Madara's senjutsu-enhanced Susanoo with a tail,[63] and with the Six Paths Chakra, battle Sasuke's Six Paths Complete Body — Susanoo to a standstill.[64] Kurama is deceptively fast, able to track Naruto in Sage Mode.[65] The fox is also very durable, enough to withstand several senjutsu-enhanced attacks,[66] and block a Tailed Beast Ball from the Ten-Tails' initial form.[67]
Ninjutsu
Kurama supercharges a Tailed Beast Ball even on low chakra.

Kurama supercharges a Tailed Beast Ball even on low chakra.

As a tailed beast, Kurama can create a Tailed Beast Ball, and fire it either as a beam or rapid blast of spheres. Kurama can also supercharge it, creating one many times its own size for much greater power, even on wisps of chakra. One of these was powerful enough to counter a collaborated Tailed Beast Ball from five tailed beasts.[55][7] At full power, Kurama's Tailed Beast Ball could blast Toneri Ōtsutsuki's giant golem through the moon and into orbit.[68] With unity between the jinchūriki and itself, Kurama can manifest itself outside for a period of time, allowing the two to fight independently and away from each other.
Kurama accumulating natural energy.

Kurama accumulating natural energy.

Kurama can unleash destructive roars, capable of dispersing a massive Rasengan, knocking back five tailed beasts at once, and destroying entire city blocks.[69][55] Even when limited through Naruto's Version 1 and 2 states, they were strong enough to destroy an entire bridge and repel Pain's Shinra Tensei respectively.[70][71] Kurama also has adept sensor skills, able to detect chakra from great distances and uniquely able to sense negative emotions,[72] as well as natural energy, which it could accumulate and transfer to Naruto to create senjutsu chakra.[73] In the anime, it is shown using Wind and Fire Release nature transformations, being able to generate twisters and breathe fire.[74]
Intelligence

Kurama is very cunning and could formulate strategies in the midst of battle. Through Naruto's body, the fox tricked Obito into using Kamui to absorb Kakashi into Kamui's dimension, only for Obito to become vulnerable to Kakashi's attacks as he phased through Naruto's punch.[75] Kurama has also shown remarkable leadership skills, being able to calmly and effectively instruct its allies on how best to engage the Ten-Tails.[76] In the anime, it has enough knowledge of fūinjutsu to determine the natures and weaknesses of the Eight Trigrams Seal that bound it inside Naruto.[23] In addition, Kurama has an advanced understanding of nuclear physics, particularly the nuclear fusion that occurs within the Sun.[60]
Part I
Prologue — Land of Waves

Main article: Prologue — Land of Waves
Naruto's first use of the Nine-Tails' chakra.

Naruto's first use of the Nine-Tails' chakra.

At the Land of Waves, Naruto and Sasuke were battling against Haku while being trapped in his Demonic Mirroring Ice Crystals technique. Haku attempted to kill Naruto, but Sasuke used his body as a shield to protect Naruto, causing him to collapse. Enraged by Sasuke's apparent death, Naruto unknowingly tapped into the Nine-Tails' chakra for the first time. In his newly-attained form, Naruto became strong enough to destroy the ice mirrors and defeat Haku.

The Nine-Tails itself expressed great delight at Naruto finally making use of its power.[23]
Chūnin Exams

Main article: Chūnin Exams Within the Forest of Death, Team 7 was ambushed by a disguised Orochimaru and his large snake summon. Realising Naruto was the Nine-Tails' jinchūriki, Orochimaru countered by using the Five Elements Seal on him, which disrupted the flow of both the fox's and Naruto's chakra. Despite the impairment, Naruto was able to beat Kiba Inuzuka and Akamaru during the preliminary rounds.

Later, during his training to prepare for the finals, Naruto encountered Jiraiya, whom the Nine-Tails recognised as the Fourth Hokage's teacher.[23] After Jiraiya deduced who Naruto was and believed it was time for Naruto to learn how to take advantage of the Nine-Tails' power, he removed Orochimaru's seal and offered to teach Naruto the Summoning Technique.
The Nine-Tails meets Naruto.

The Nine-Tails meets Naruto.

As the month of training was nearing its end, Naruto was still struggling to summon large toads. In order to accelerate Naruto's slow progress, Jiraiya, without warning, pushed him off a cliff. In his act of desperation, Naruto, for the first time, abruptly entered his subconscious and encountered the Nine-Tails, who showed no hesitation in expressing both its hostility and desire to devour Naruto. Despite initially being terrified, Naruto boldly demanded that the Nine-Tails give him its chakra as a form of "rent money" for living in his body, since his death would also result in the fox's own. Realising this, the amused Nine-Tails complied, giving Naruto enough chakra to summon Gamabunta.

With Naruto finally becoming aware of its presence, the scheming Nine-Tails was convinced that it could influence him even more to use its power.[23]

At the time of the finals, the first match was Naruto facing Neji Hyūga. After Neji sealed his tenketsu with Sixty-Four Palms, Naruto called upon the Nine-Tails for aid. Without hesitating, the fox gave Naruto enough chakra to force his tenketsu to open and defeat Neji.
Konoha Crush

Main article: Konoha Crush During the climax of Naruto's fight against Gaara, the Nine-Tails gave some of its chakra to Naruto, leading to him defeating Shukaku's jinchūriki.
Search for Tsunade

Main article: Search for Tsunade While on his search for Tsunade with Jiraiya, Naruto was confronted by Itachi Uchiha and Kisame Hoshigaki of Akatsuki. Naruto tried to use the Nine-Tails' chakra to help him fight against them, but Kisame quickly used his Samehada sword to absorb it all and attempted to cut off Naruto's arms. Luckily, Jiraiya arrived in time to force both Akatsuki members to retreat.

Later, in the midst of battle against Orochimaru's right-hand man Kabuto Yakushi, Naruto managed to defeat him with a perfected Rasengan. However, Naruto collapsed as Kabuto in turn severed his heart muscles and neighbouring chakra system, to which the Nine-Tails was unable to heal. Within Naruto's subconscious, the fox quickly noticed the surrounding light was fading as well as its strength, indicating that both its life and Naruto's were on the brink. Fortunately, both Naruto and the Nine-Tails were able to survive with the help of Tsunade and her medical ninjutsu.
Sasuke Recovery Mission

Main article: Sasuke Recovery Mission
The Nine-Tails' chakra manifests as an image of itself.

The Nine-Tails' chakra manifests as an image of itself.

Naruto, determined to prevent Sasuke from severing ties to Konoha, managed to catch up to him at the Valley of the End, and they began their fight. Just as Sasuke, enhanced by his cursed seal, pierced through Naruto's right shoulder, the Nine-Tails quickly gave Naruto enough chakra to enter his initial jinchūriki form, instantly healing his large wound. Naruto initially overwhelmed Sasuke with his increased speed and strength, but as the fight continued, Sasuke's Sharingan fully matured, allowing him to predict and overcome Naruto's attacks. The Nine-Tails callously berated Naruto for being weak and said that he should be grateful to the fox before granting him more of its chakra. The dense red chakra enveloped Naruto, transforming the jinchūriki into his one-tailed form. Ultimately, the power-up wasn't enough, as Sasuke then advanced to the second level of his cursed seal, and the fight ended with Naruto lying on the ground, unconscious.
Kaima Capture Mission

Main article: Kaima Capture Mission When Naruto was overwhelmed by the Umibōzu summoned by Amachi, he entered his initial jinchūriki form, overwhelming Umibōzu and later defeating Amachi. Naruto soon after became exhausted and fell into the ocean but he was saved by Isaribi.
Interlude

During Naruto's three-year training under Jiraiya, the Sannin began training Naruto to gain better control of the Nine-Tails' chakra, soon helping Naruto to master wielding the chakra cloak up to two tails. Feeling he was ready to take it to the next level, Jiraiya had Gerotora tweak the seal enough for additional chakra to be unleashed. However, the Nine-Tails took advantage of this and began mocking Naruto for his failure to save Sasuke, able to use Naruto's negative emotions to overwhelm him with additional chakra. This ultimately caused Naruto to slip into a four-tailed Version 2 state, which allowed the Nine-Tails to go on a rampage in Naruto's body. Jiraiya was narrowly able to reseal the fox's chakra, to which his passive methods of saving Naruto led to Jiraiya being severely injured.[77]

Unable to tell friend from foe, Naruto attacked anyone around him, including Sakura. After Yamato managed to suppress the Nine-Tails' chakra, he later revealed to Naruto that he was the one responsible for Sakura's injury, which greatly horrified Naruto and resolved to never rely on the Nine-Tails' power again.
The Nine-Tails tempts Naruto.

The Nine-Tails tempts Naruto.

Naruto firmly refused, but the Nine-Tails scoffed, cruelly remarking that he couldn't do anything on his own. When the fox noted his similarity to Madara Uchiha in terms of visual prowess and chakra, Sasuke immediately responded by suppressing the fox. Although outraged by the display of his power, the Nine-Tails warned Sasuke not to kill Naruto, stating he would only regret it.

When Isshiki was going to kill Sasuke, Naruto managed to hold Isshiki off with his shadow clones while Sasuke escaped with his Rinnegan. As Naruto continued to taunt Isshiki, Kurama quickly told him off, stating that from the beginning Isshiki never had any intentions to kill him, but could just easily change his mind and slaughter them both, to which the Ōtsutsuki commended the fox before sealing them away in a giant coffin. The seal was then put under safeguard by Boro.

Determined to save them, Team 7 and Kawaki later used the power of Kawaki and Boruto's Kāma to enter the dimension. While struggling to fight against Boro, Boruto and Kawaki used their Kāma to create a rift in the seal, freeing the unconscious Naruto. After the team defeated Boro and returned to Konoha with Naruto, they were all taken to the hospital for treatment, with Naruto making a swift recovery.
Kurama preparing to activate Baryon Mode.

Kurama preparing to activate Baryon Mode.

Later, after Boruto teleported the resurrected Isshiki and himself to a separate dimension, Kurama, Naruto, and Sasuke managed to join Boruto for their second confrontation against Isshiki. However, the Ōtsutsuki once again proved too powerful as he knocked out Sasuke and blocked Naruto's sensory abilities by manifesting giant black cubes, a fact that Kurama noticed. Seeing how determined Naruto was to stop Isshiki, even if it cost him his own life, Kurama suggested using a last resort while misleading him into thinking it would result in his death. Naruto agreed to it, declaring that the moment he decided to become Hokage, he knew the risks and was prepared to die. Naruto took on a new form of Baryon Mode.

Kurama explained to Naruto that the form is like nuclear fusion, consuming all their respective energy and that he must be careful not to make any unnecessary movements or thoughts. The new form baffled everyone with its sheer power. Naruto quickly began overwhelming Isshiki. Soon after, the strain of Baryon Mode began catching up as Naruto began to tire. Kurama noted this form gradually drains all life-forces, including Naruto's. However, it would also drain Isshiki's with each contact, meaning they just had to keep pressuring Isshiki until his already diminished lifespan ran out.
Kurama's life fades away after a final conversation with Naruto.

Kurama's life fades away after a final conversation with Naruto.

After Isshiki's defeat, Kurama spoke with Naruto inside his subconscious for a final conversation as Baryon Mode proved too much to handle. In the manga, Naruto told the kitsune that he had no ill will to Kurama despite killing his parents. In the anime, they reminisce about their history together as Naruto prepared to die. However, Kurama revealed that Naruto would be fine, as it was Kurama's life that was gambled from using Baryon Mode. Kurama revealed the double meaning behind its earlier words and Naruto interpreted it as Baryon Mode resulting in his death rather than Kurama's. Before Kurama's life finally faded away, it warned Naruto that he would lose all access to Kurama's chakra and abilities, meaning he would have to be more careful from now on.

Soon after, the remaining kernels of Kurama's chakra found its way into Himawari.
New Era: Part II
Boruto's Return Arc

Main article: Boruto's Return Arc Three years after its temporary death, Kurama is revived as a much smaller, younger form. Its presence drew the attention of the Human God Trees, Jura and Hidari, who had mistaken Himawari for her father. As Team 10 retreated with Himawari from the God Trees, Kurama reached out to her within her mental realm, surprised that incarnations of the Ten-Tails were able to sense it.

As Kurama introduced itself, it explained the situation to Himawari. Kurama surmised that its rebirth inside her came from her unique heritage, both being born of two strong bloodlines and inheriting some of its chakra already as the daughter of Kurama's previous jinchūriki. Himawari merely welcomed the fox, impressing Kurama at her maturity and acceptance of the situation. It also revealed to Himawari's compatibility to the fox's power is even greater than her father's was. As Himawari regained consciousness, Jura caught up to Himawari and forced them to the ground. When Inojin was impaled by Jura's Wood Release, the sight of her maimed friend enraged Himawari enough to access Kurama's power. Proud of how quickly Himawari adapted, it encouraged her to fight. Using her newfound abilities, Himawari healed herself and Inojin. She then proceeded to pummel Jura about, until the God Tree decided to take the fight more seriously and swiftly subdued her, prompting Kurama to admit that Himawari could not win. Instead, Boruto arrived to help his sister and teleported them away.

Trivia
Springtime of Youth
	The Springtime of Youth wiki has an article about this topic:
Nine-Tails

    Kurama was the first character shown in the series.
    "Kurama" (九喇嘛) literally means "nine lama". Kishimoto was mainly inspired to create Kurama based on the character with the same name from the manga series Yū Yū Hakusho.[79] The name may also reference Mount Kurama (鞍馬山, Kurama-yama), the sacred mountain said to be the home of Sōjōbō, a tengu who taught the arts of swordsmanship to Minamoto no Yoshitsune.
        Furthermore, in the jōruri and kabuki play Yoshitsune and the Thousand Cherry Trees, Minamoto no Yoshitsune is accompanied by Genkurō, a kitsune disguising himself as Yoshitsune's retainer, Satō Tadanobu.
    Kitsune (狐, fox) are popular animal yōkai in Japanese folklore, and are very well-known to be cunning tricksters with shape-shifting abilities, to which on occasions, use to transform themselves into beautiful women in order to seduce men (possibly the inspiration for the Sexy Technique; Naruto even thought Kushina would be Kurama, when they first met). They are also said to be rivals to the tanuki, which created an old Japanese proverb: "A fox and a tanuki matching their cleverness" (狐と狸の化かし合い, kitsune to tanuki no bakashi ai), which meant the two sly characters trying to outsmart one another. However, kitsune are known to keep their promises, a trait Naruto has with his nindō. Stories often depict kitsune growing additional tails as they age, which increases their magical power; once a kitsune reaches 1,000 years of age and grows their ninth and final tail, their fur turns gold and becomes a tenko (天狐, celestial fox), making themselves equivalent to a kami.
    In the manga pilot, Kurama was actually Naruto's father, who attacked the village of Mt. Oinari and required nine shinobi to defeat him. Hiruzen was the only survivor and had taken pity on Naruto, who had taken human form, and allowed him to live in the village.
    Kurama's death was featured in Boruto episode 218, on the 19th anniversary of the debut of the Naruto anime, where Kurama debuted.
    Despite being a kitsune in appearance, Kurama has never once been called the Kyubi no Kitsune in the official translation, but is erroneously called that in the English anime and Viz translations.
